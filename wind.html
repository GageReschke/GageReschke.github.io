Overview

This project set out to determine which of five Northeastern University campus locations experiences the highest wind speed. Using two co-located HT9830 anemometers — one stationary control (A1) and one field unit (A2) — we collected 18 data points per location across 3 trials and 6 time intervals, then applied a rigorous 7-phase statistical analysis to rank the sites.

The five locations measured were IV, West Village, Marino, Krentzman Quad, and Snell Engineering Center.

Methodology

01
Sensor Calibration
Both anemometers co-located to collect 18 paired readings. A paired t-test confirmed systematic bias; a correction factor of +0.2561 m/s was derived and applied to all A2 field readings.
02
Corrected Data & Wind Speed Ratio
A normalized Wind Speed Ratio (Corrected A2 / A1 Control) was computed for each reading to isolate location effect from ambient weather variability across measurement days.
03
Outlier Detection — Chauvenet's Criterion
Applied independently to corrected wind speed and normalized ratio at all five locations. Flagged outliers at IV, West Village, Marino, and Snell — all retained per standard practice.
04
Uncertainty Analysis (95% CI)
Combined instrument uncertainty (resolution + calibration accuracy) with statistical spread to compute 95% confidence intervals per location. Wide CIs at West Village (±1.406 m/s) reflect genuine gustiness, not measurement error.
05
One-Way ANOVA
Run on both corrected WS (F=7.74, p=0.000023) and normalized ratio (F=4.18, p=0.004). Both rejected H₀ — location significantly affects wind speed at α=0.05.
06
Pairwise Welch's t-Tests
10 pairwise comparisons run with unequal variance assumption. IV and West Village were significantly windier than Krentzman and Snell. Snell was significantly different from all other locations by normalized ratio.
07
Final Rankings
Both metrics ranked West Village #1. The normalized ratio was used as the primary metric since it controls for ambient weather variation across measurement sessions.

Key Finding

West Village is the windiest location by both corrected wind speed (4.57 m/s) and normalized ratio (2.69), confirmed statistically significant versus Krentzman and Snell. Marino ranks 2nd by normalized ratio but 3rd by raw speed — indicating its field wind was strong relative to ambient conditions on its measurement day. The normalized ratio is the more reliable metric as it controls for day-to-day weather variability.

Sources of Error

  • Locations measured on different days — ambient weather varied. The WS ratio partially corrects for this but micro-weather effects cannot be fully eliminated
  • Sensor placement height and horizontal position may vary slightly between trials, introducing wind shear error
  • Manual triggering of readings at 10-second intervals adds human timing variability — an automated logger would reduce this noise
  • Flagged outliers at multiple locations inflate standard deviation and widen confidence intervals
